# Evaluation Framework — Go/No-Go Scoring by Contract Type

## Universal Scoring Rubric (1-5 per dimension)

### 1. Capability Fit
| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Perfect match — we've done this exact scope multiple times |
| 4 | Strong match — we have 80%+ of required skills in-house |
| 3 | Moderate match — we can do it with minor gaps to fill |
| 2 | Weak match — significant skill gaps, would need heavy subcontracting |
| 1 | Poor match — outside our core competency entirely |

### 2. Timeline Feasibility
| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Comfortable timeline, we can staff it today |
| 4 | Tight but achievable with normal resource allocation |
| 3 | Achievable with some team stretching or light overtime |
| 2 | Very tight — risk of penalties if anything goes wrong |
| 1 | Unrealistic — cannot deliver in time given current commitments |

### 3. Financial Attractiveness
| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | High value, excellent margin potential (25%+), fast payment terms |
| 4 | Good value, solid margin (15-25%), standard payment terms |
| 3 | Acceptable value, thin margin (8-15%), or slow payment terms |
| 2 | Low margin (below 8%) or requires significant upfront investment |
| 1 | Loss-leader or unclear margin; not worth it |

### 4. Competition Risk
| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | We have a unique position — few competitors can match our offer |
| 4 | We are among the top 3 likely bidders |
| 3 | Competitive field but we have a credible shot |
| 2 | Highly competitive — many qualified bidders, thin differentiation |
| 1 | We would be bidding against entrenched incumbents with no advantage |

### 5. Risk Level
| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Low risk — clear scope, good client history, standard terms |
| 4 | Manageable risk — minor uncertainties, reasonable penalties |
| 3 | Moderate risk — some scope ambiguity or strict performance clauses |
| 2 | High risk — unclear scope, aggressive penalties, new client |
| 1 | Very high risk — vague scope, unlimited liability, no payment history |

### 6. Strategic Value
| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Opens a major new client, sector, or geographic market |
| 4 | Strengthens existing relationship or adds important reference |
| 3 | Useful addition to portfolio but not transformational |
| 2 | Minimal strategic value beyond the contract itself |
| 1 | No strategic value; purely transactional |

## Thresholds by Contract Type

### IT / Software / Consulting
- Minimum viable: 18/30
- Capability fit weight: HIGH (if score < 3, strong reconsider regardless of total)
- Key concern: Unrealistic timelines are common in government IT

### Construction / Civil Works
- Minimum viable: 20/30
- Financial attractiveness weight: HIGH (margins are thin; watch cash flow)
- Key concern: Fianzas, retenciones, and slow payment can destroy cash flow

### Supplies / Products
- Minimum viable: 16/30
- Competition risk weight: HIGH (price is usually the only differentiator)
- Key concern: Ensure you can actually source at the price you bid

### Professional Services
- Minimum viable: 18/30
- Strategic value weight: MEDIUM (government reference clients are valuable)
- Key concern: Scope creep is rampant in service contracts; define deliverables tightly

## Red-Flag Checklist

Before any GO decision, verify:

**Legal / Administrative:**
- [ ] Tender is in a sector where your company is registered to operate
- [ ] You meet the financial capacity requirements (capital contable, ventas mínimas)
- [ ] You have or can obtain the required insurance and bonds
- [ ] No conflict of interest with the contracting agency
- [ ] Submission deadline is realistic (minimum 5 business days to prepare)

**Technical:**
- [ ] Technical requirements are fully understood (no ambiguous specs)
- [ ] Required certifications are current
- [ ] Subcontractors are identified and confirmed (if needed)
- [ ] Site visit has been done or scheduled (for construction)

**Financial:**
- [ ] Cash flow supports the contract (can you fund operations before first payment?)
- [ ] Margin covers minimum acceptable return after all costs
- [ ] Payment terms won't create a liquidity problem

**Strategic:**
- [ ] Not in a "disqualified bidder" situation from prior contracts
- [ ] No reputational risk from this client or project

## Common Traps to Avoid

1. **Bases trap:** Requirements that seem neutral but effectively pre-select a specific company (e.g., "experience with system X from company Y"). Flag and consider whether this is winnable.

2. **Scope ambiguity:** Government agencies often write vague specs. If you can't price it confidently, request aclaraciones before the deadline.

3. **Precio no creíble:** Bids more than 30-40% below competitors' estimated range may be disqualified. Don't race to the bottom.

4. **Fianza timing:** Some tenders require fianzas at the time of bid submission, not just at contract signing. Confirm with your bonding company in advance.

5. **Registro en padrón:** Many dependencias require registration in their vendor registry (padrón de proveedores) before you can even bid. Check this first.
