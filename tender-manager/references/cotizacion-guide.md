# Cotización Guide — Cost Estimation by Sector

## General Principles

### Overhead Rates (% of direct costs) — Mexico Government Contracts
| Company size | Admin overhead | Recommended |
|---|---|---|
| Micro (1-10 employees) | 8-12% | 10% |
| Small (11-50) | 10-15% | 12% |
| Medium (51-250) | 12-18% | 15% |
| Large (250+) | 15-22% | 18% |

### Profit Margins — Government Work
| Contract type | Low | Target | High |
|---|---|---|---|
| Simple supplies/products | 10% | 18% | 25% |
| Professional services | 20% | 30% | 40% |
| Construction/civil | 12% | 20% | 28% |
| IT/software | 25% | 35% | 45% |
| Consulting | 30% | 40% | 50% |

### Contingency Buffer
- Standard: 5%
- Complex technical: 8%
- First-time client or risky scope: 10%
- International or cross-border: 12%

## By Sector

### IT & Software Services

**Labor rates (MXN/day, 2025-2026 market rates):**
| Role | Junior | Mid | Senior |
|---|---|---|---|
| Developer | $1,200 | $2,000 | $3,500 |
| Project Manager | $1,500 | $2,500 | $4,000 |
| Analyst / Consultant | $1,200 | $2,200 | $3,800 |
| QA / Tester | $900 | $1,500 | $2,500 |
| UX/UI Designer | $1,000 | $1,800 | $3,000 |
| DevOps / Infra | $1,500 | $2,500 | $4,500 |

**Typical IT project cost structure:**
- Labor: 60-70%
- Licenses/software: 10-20%
- Infrastructure/cloud: 5-15%
- Admin overhead: 10-15%

**Government IT specifics:**
- Add 5% for documentation requirements (manuals, reports, actas)
- Add 2-3% for government security/compliance requirements
- Factor training sessions into labor hours

### Supplies & Products

**Pricing approach:**
1. Get 3 supplier quotes
2. Use best quote as base cost
3. Add logistics: 5-8%
4. Add storage/warehousing: 2-5%
5. Add margin: 15-25%

**Watch for:** Unit price vs. total price consistency — government auditors check both.

## IVA Rules

- **IVA (16%)** applies to most goods and services
- **IVA exento:** Some educational services, primary food items
- Always show prices both WITH and WITHOUT IVA in proposals
- Government clients pay IVA separately — don't hide it in the price

## Payment Terms in Government Contracts

Typical structure:
- Anticipo: 20-30% at contract signing (requires fianza de anticipo)
- Pagos parciales: upon delivery of agreed milestones (estimaciones)
- Finiquito: 10% held until formal acceptance (acta de entrega-recepción)

**Cash flow impact:** Budget for 60-90 day payment delays — government agencies are often slow.
Factor in financing cost if you need to pre-fund operations.

---

## Red Flags — When to Reprice

- Your price is more than 20% below the tender reference value → may signal you're underpricing or misunderstanding scope
- Your price is more than 30% above → may be uncompetitive; review assumptions
- Single largest cost item is more than 60% of total → concentration risk, revisit
- Labor hours seem too low for the scope → add buffer or clarify scope before submitting
